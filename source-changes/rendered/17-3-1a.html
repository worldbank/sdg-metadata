2.a. Definition and concepts (STAT_CONC_DEF)

Concepts and definitions

Definition:

FDI part: FDI inflows (expressed in millions of US dollars)

Concepts:

Foreign direct investment (FDI) is defined as an investment involving a long-term relationship 

and reflecting a lasting interest and control by a resident entity in one economy (foreign

direct investor or parent enterprise) in an enterprise resident in an economy other than 

that of the foreign direct investor (FDI enterprise or affiliate enterprise or foreign affiliate). Please see the Methodological Note of UNCTAD’s World Investment Report (

https://unctad.org/ensystem/PublicationChaptersfiles/wir2019chMethodNote_enofficial-document/wir2020chMethodNote_en.pdf).

3.a. Data sources (SOURCE_TYPE)

Data sources

Description:

UNCTAD has been collecting FDI data by relevant national authorities, such as central banks and statistical offices (please see here for the sources and methodology of data for each economy (https://unctad.org/ensystem/PublicationChaptersfiles/wir2019chMethodNote_enofficial-document/wir2020chMethodNote_en.pdf).

3.b. Data collection method (COLL_METHOD)

Collection process:

FDI data: In compiling FDI inflows published data and websites of national sources 

were consulted first. Requests were then made to national official sources of all economies 

for verification and/or updates and supplementary data.

4.a. Rationale (RATIONALE)

Rationale:

FDI has been the largest source of external finance for developing economies over the past decade, and the most resilient to economic and financial shocks.

4.b. Comment and limitations (REC_USE_LIM)

Comments and limitations:

FDI inflows are available from 1980 onwards.

4.c. Method of computation (DATA_COMP)

Methodology

Computation method:

UNCTAD regularly collects published and unpublished national official FDI data flows 

directly from central banks, statistical offices or national authorities. 

4.f. Treatment of missing values (i) at country level and (ii) at regional level (IMPUTATION)

Treatment of missing values:

  • At country level:

FDI data: estimated by annualizing the data or using mirror data.

  • At regional and global levels:

FDI data: None
